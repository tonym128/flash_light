How It Works & Setup Tips

📷 The Physics of Rolling Shutter

Smartphone cameras read out the pixel sensor row-by-row (rolling shutter) rather than all at once. When pointing at a flickering light bulb, different rows are exposed at slightly different times. This translates the temporal oscillation of the light into a spatial pattern of dark and bright bands across the frame. By counting these bands relative to the sensor's readout speed, we calculate the exact frequency (Hz).

⚙️ How to Get High-Precision Readings

  • Increase shutter speed (manual exposure): If exposure is too long, the bands blur out. To force your phone's camera into high shutter speed, point it directly at a bright light bulb. This forces the camera's auto-exposure to use a very fast shutter speed (under 1ms), producing extremely sharp bands.
  • Hold the phone still: Keep the phone steady or lean it against a surface so that hand movement doesn't disrupt the spatial bands.
  • Perform the Auto-Calibration: Every phone's camera sensor has a slightly different row readout speed (rolling shutter skew). Point at a standard non-LED incandescent or fluorescent bulb (which fluctuates at exactly double your AC grid frequency: 100Hz or 120Hz) and press Auto Calibrate to save your phone's exact profile!
